About the Destination

A Nation Forged
by the Sea

Scattered across the western Pacific like emeralds on sapphire silk, the Philippines is an archipelago of 7,641 islands — each one a world unto itself.

From Palawan's UNESCO-listed underground rivers to Boracay's legendary white-sand shores, from Cebu's Spanish-colonial heritage to Siargao's world-class surf breaks, the Philippines delivers a travel experience as diverse as it is breathtaking. Warm locals, extraordinary cuisine, and the planet's most biodiverse coral reefs make this Southeast Asian gem one of history's greatest island destinations.

Top Islands to Explore

Five legendary destinations. Five completely different adventures.

  • Editor's Pick

    Palawan

    Home to the Puerto Princesa Underground River — a UNESCO World Heritage Site — and the jaw-dropping lagoons of El Nido and Coron. Often called the most beautiful island on earth.

    • ⛵ Island Hopping
    • 🤿 Diving
  • Most Popular

    Boracay

    World-renowned White Beach stretches 4 km of powdery talcum-white sand. The perfect blend of relaxation, water sports, and vibrant nightlife in the heart of the Visayas.

    • 🏄 Water Sports
    • 🌅 Sunsets

  • Cebu

    The Queen City of the South blends 500 years of Spanish colonial heritage with pristine beaches, whale shark encounters in Oslob, and the stunning Kawasan Canyon waterfalls.

    • 🦈 Whale Sharks
    • 🏛 Heritage

  • Bohol

    Famous for the otherworldly Chocolate Hills — over 1,200 cone-shaped mounds — and the world's smallest primate, the tarsier. Panglao Island's white beaches are a short bridge away.

    • 🌿 Nature
    • 🐒 Wildlife
  • Surf Capital

    Siargao

    The surf capital of the Philippines draws riders from around the world to Cloud 9. Beyond the waves: turquoise lagoons, mangrove forests, rock pools, and pure island soul.

    • 🏄 Surfing
    • 🌊 Lagoons
